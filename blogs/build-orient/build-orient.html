Introduction

Additive Manufacturing (AM) enables the creation of complex geometries with customizable material properties, revolutionizing fields such as design and manufacturing. However, the layered nature of FDM introduces anisotropy, which significantly affects the mechanical performance of printed parts. This research addresses these challenges by proposing a build orientation optimization framework that leverages machine learning to enhance structural strength. The algorithm optimizes the orientation to maximize the minimum Factor of Safety (FoS) under specified loading conditions, ensuring even stress distribution and improved mechanical integrity.

Methodology

The proposed optimization framework consists of four primary stages: Physical Experiments, Virtual Simulation, Machine Learning, and Optimization. The process begins with characterizing the material properties through physical experiments, which are then used to calibrate a Finite Element Model (FEM) for virtual simulations. Machine learning, specifically Artificial Neural Networks (ANN), is employed to approximate the complex relationships observed in the data, allowing for faster optimization using Bayesian algorithms.

Results

The optimization framework was tested on various components, including standard tensile samples and complex geometries such as a wheel hub and door handle. In each scenario, the optimized build orientation led to significant improvements in mechanical strength, with tensile strength increasing by up to 126% in some cases. The computational model accurately predicted the optimal orientations, aligning the strongest build layers with the loading paths, thus enhancing the overall performance of the parts.

The framework's efficacy was validated through both computational simulations and physical testing. Components fabricated using the optimal orientations showed markedly improved structural integrity compared to those printed in default orientations. This confirms the practical applicability of the proposed machine learning-driven optimization approach.

Conclusion

This study demonstrates the potential of machine learning-based optimization in enhancing the mechanical performance of FDM components by optimizing build orientation. The integrated approach combining physical experiments, FEM simulations, and machine learning provides a robust tool for determining optimal build orientations, which significantly improves structural strength.
